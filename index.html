About Us

Baga Saraswat Gyan Niketan, established in 2010, is dedicated to improving education in rural and marginalized areas, particularly for children from Scheduled Caste (BPL) families. Their "Green Color Identity" project focuses on bridging the educational gap for children lagging in basic literacy by using a micro-teaching method. By providing personalized attention in small groups, the society helps these children catch up before entering higher grades. Through this initiative, the society aims to create a lasting impact on underprivileged children‚Äôs education and appeals for support to expand the project further.

Our Journey

Established on 26th January 2010, Baga Saraswat Gyan Niketan has been a beacon of hope for rural students in Bankura district. Registered under the West Bengal Societies Registration Act (Reg. No. SO173334 of 2010-2011), our society is driven by a dedicated team of educators and volunteers working to bridge the gap in early childhood education. Over the last decade, we've initiated projects like "Green Color Identity" to bring educational justice to backward, SC, and BPL communities through micro-teaching and support.

About Us

Baga Saraswat Gyan Niketan, established under the West Bengal Societies Registration Act XXVI of 1961, is dedicated to improving the quality of education for children, especially in rural and backward areas. Our organization aims to provide primary education to children, focusing on marginalized communities, including Scheduled Caste and BPL families. We believe in empowering communities through education and social awareness.

Mission & Vision

Mission: To provide quality education to every child in rural areas and empower marginalized communities through education.

Vision: To create an inclusive society where every child, regardless of their background, has access to quality education and opportunities for growth and development.
